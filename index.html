Materials and Costs

I have ordered the Riverside paperback editions for each play at the campus bookstore. You can get them as Kindle or e-books for about one dollar apiece, or buy them on Amazon for about three dollars each. These books are inexpensive and contain helpful glosses and illustrations.

If you already own a comparable edition with textual notes, feel free to use that. Do not use full-text editions without glosses as they won’t be sufficient for exams and writing assignments.

Watching Films

Films, like the readings, are your responsibility outside of class. There are due dates for watching the films on the course schedule. You should always read the play first before watching to focus on analyzing directorial choices rather than following the plot.

All required films are on reserve at Galvin Library. Simply provide the call number at the circulation desk. You can also bring your laptop and headphones to watch them there or reserve a group study room.

If needed, films are available for rent on platforms like Amazon or iTunes for a small fee.

Objectives

  • Understand the context of Shakespeare's plays for Elizabethan audiences.
  • Develop knowledge of terminology related to film/stage/print drama.
  • Analyze different productions of Shakespeare's works and perform detailed written analysis.
  • Use primary/secondary sources for textual analysis.
  • Evaluate and justify intellectual claims using evidence.

Assignments

Scene Analysis (15%)

Analyze one scene from two plays on the course blog. See the assignment description for details.

Midterm Exam (15%)

Comprehensive exam covering plays, films, and critical terminology.

Play Review (15%)

Write a review of Posner and Teller's The Tempest. Your review must be supported by course concepts.

Final Project (25%)

Analyze a film covered in the course and discuss how it interprets Shakespeare’s text, using secondary sources.

Final Exam (15%)

A comprehensive exam covering readings, films, and course concepts.

Participation (15%)

Active participation in class discussions and attendance are critical.
